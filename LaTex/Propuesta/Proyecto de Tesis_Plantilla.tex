\section{Revisión de Literatura}
\subsection{Monitoring of linear profiles}

A standard assumption in the monitoring of simple linear regression profiles is that the errors are independent and identically distributed, usually with an assumed normal distribution, \citeasnoun{Woodall_2007}. \citeasnoun{Soleimani_Noorossana_2007}, \citeasnoun{Noorossana_Amiri_2008} and \citeasnoun{Soleimani_Noorossana_Amiri_2009} studied the effect of ignoring autocorrelation of the errors. Autocorrelated errors frequently occur within profile monitoring data because successive measurements are often made close in space and/or time. Noorossana et al. (2004b) studied the effect of non-normality of the error terms, using t-distributions as alternatives, on the performance of the EWMA/R method of Kang and Albin (2000). \citeasnoun{Noorossana_Saghaei_Dorri_2010} studied the effect of non-normality on profile monitoring in Phase II when within or between autocorrelation is present.

\subsection{Process Capability Analysis}

A process can be viewed as a series of actions or operations influenced by several elements or factors, all contributing to the eventual outcome. These elements or causes of variation can be generally broken into the following typical categories: Material, machine, method, manpower, environment and measurement, \citeasnoun{Rezaie_Ostadi_Taghizadeh_2006}.
